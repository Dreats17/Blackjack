## The Casino

### The Dealer

The central NPC of the game. A mysterious figure with a **jade glass eye** who runs the Blackjack table.

**Characteristics:**
- Speaks in terse, cryptic sentences
- Has a hidden backstory involving his wife Maria and daughter Sofia (revealed in the Dealer's Past storyline)
- His mood (happiness 0–100) directly affects gameplay
- Can and will **kill you** if sufficiently angered
- Carries a revolver

**Behaviors by Happiness:**
- **High (90–100):** May give free hands, pleasant demeanor
- **Medium (30–89):** Normal behavior
- **Low (20–29):** Visible anger, warnings
- **Critical (0–9):** May shoot you dead or eject you

**Gift System:**
- You can wrap items at Kyle's store and deliver them at the casino
- Gifts modify Dealer happiness (−50 to +30)
- Some gifts are **lethal** (he shoots you)

## Kyle's Convenience Store

### Kyle (NPC)

A convenience store clerk who becomes a recurring presence. He has **30+ condition-reactive greetings** — he reacts differently based on your:
- Current injuries and illnesses
- Mental state and sanity
- Items you're carrying
- Wealth level
- How often you visit

### Services

**Shopping:**
- One-item limit per visit (40% chance to buy a second item)
- Inventory is **tiered by wealth rank** — richer players see different stock
- Always has food items available

**Tier-Specific Inventory Highlights:**
| Rank | Notable Items |
|------|--------------|
| Poor | Candy Bar, Bag of Chips, Energy Drink, Lighter, Matches |
| Cheap | Turkey Sandwich, Beef Jerky, First Aid Kit, Flashlight |
| Modest | Cup Noodles, Granola Bar, Blanket, Sunglasses |
| Rich | Necronomicon ($666), Cursed Coin ($13), Lottery Ticket |
| Doughman+ | Premium items, rare consumables |

**Gift Wrapping:**
- Unlocks after 3–5 purchases at rank ≥ 1
- Costs $10 per wrap
- Wrap any inventory item to give to the Dealer
- Wrapped gift is automatically delivered next casino visit

**Storyline:** Kyle has a 5-part storyline arc where you learn about his stressed life, his mom's medical bills, and his scheme to skim from the register. You can choose to help or expose him.

## Doctor's Office

### Services

- **Full heal** to 100 HP
- **Clears all illness and injury statuses**
- **Restores 1–3 sanity** as a bonus
- **Cost:** 65–90% of your current balance

### Insurance Items

| Item | Effect on Cost |
|------|---------------|
| None | 65–90% of balance |
| **Faulty Insurance** | 10–35% of balance (but 10% chance of being banned from future visits) |
| **Real Insurance** (upgraded) | **Free** — no cost at all |

> **Tip:** Real Insurance is one of the most valuable upgrades in the game. It eliminates the massive drain of doctor visits entirely.

## Witch Doctor's Tower

### Services

**Status Curing:**
- 50% chance to clear **one** random status effect (illness/injury)
- 10% chance to clear **all** status effects at once

**Flask Shop:**
Each visit, flasks have a **66% chance** of being in stock. Available flasks:

| Flask | Price | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| No Bust | Varies | Prevents busting during Blackjack |
| Second Chance | Varies | Replay one losing hand per session |
| Split Serum | Varies | Grants Split action |
| Dealer's Hesitation | Varies | Force dealer extra hit 66% of the time |
| Bonus Fortune | Varies | Grants Double Down action |
| Pocket Aces | Varies | First card guaranteed Ace (one-time) |
| Anti-Venom | Varies | Cures poison statuses |
| Anti-Virus | Varies | Cures illness statuses |
| Fortunate Day | Varies | Better day event outcomes |
| Fortunate Night | Varies | Better night event outcomes |
| Imminent Blackjack | Varies | Increased Blackjack chances |
| Dealer's Whispers | Varies | Hints at dealer's hidden card |

### Flask Mixing Warning

Having **3+ active flask effects** simultaneously:
- **50%:** Puke up all effects
- **10–40 damage**
- **Possible death**

## Tom's Trusty Trucks & Tires

### Tom (NPC)

A kind, folksy mechanic with an evolving personality. He gets to know you over multiple visits.

**Personality:** Warm, paternal, uses colloquialisms ("yunno"), genuinely cares about your well-being.

**First Visit:**
- Introduction scene with shop description
- Cost: $150–$350

**Subsequent Visits:**
- Evolving dialogue (0–3+ visits unlocks deeper conversation)
- Repair services at $4,000–$17,500 per item
- 50% success rate on repairs
- 20–35% chance of discount

### Dream Chain (Tom Path)

Tom triggers a 3-part dream sequence:
1. **Part 1:** You dream of a woman named Rebecca
2. **Part 2:** You dream of a boy named Nathan — your son
3. **Part 3:** Revelation: "Your name is Johnathan"

These dreams set up the **Salvation ending** — the "best" ending where you go home to your family.

## Frank's Filthy Repair

### Frank (NPC)

A crude, xenophobic mechanic who goes on political rants and insults you freely.

**Personality:** Offensive, unprofessional, cheap. His shop is filthy. He makes racist and sexist jokes. He's terrible at his job but cheap.

**First Visit:**
- Cost: $50–$100
- 40% success rate (worse than other mechanics)

**Subsequent Visits:**
- Repair services at $3,000–$25,000
- 50% success rate
- No discounts
- Crude failure dialogue
- Political rants between repairs

### Dream Chain (Frank Path)

Frank triggers a darker 3-part dream sequence:
1. **Part 1:** You dream of the Dealer's anger
2. **Part 2:** You see the Dealer's scar — his jade glass eye up close
3. **Part 3:** The Dealer pulls his revolver and fires — **BANG**

These dreams explore the Dealer's violent nature and your fear of him.

## Oswald's Workshop

### Oswald (NPC)

A manic, brilliant businessman-inventor with **Stuart** (a young apprentice with cyborg hands) working under him.

**Personality:** Energetic, capitalistic, slightly unhinged. Treats everything as a business opportunity. Genuinely talented.

**First Visit:**
- Expensive introduction: $500,000 → negotiated to $800–$900
- Stuart shows you around

### Services

**Repair:**
- 50% success rate
- Sometimes gives tips or free money on the side
- Cost varies

**UPGRADE SHOP (Main Feature):**
Requires 3+ total mechanic visits across all mechanics.

Oswald can upgrade any of the **17 base items** to their superior versions:
- **Cost:** $120,000–$400,000 per upgrade
- All 17 upgrades are listed in the [Items Guide](04_ITEMS_FLASKS_AND_UPGRADES.md#upgradeable-items-17-items)

### The Posthuman Ending

When ALL 17 items are upgraded, Oswald reveals:
- You're "not human anymore" — you've transcended
- He opens the "Grand Casino"
- Triggers the **Eternity ending** — a unique game conclusion

### Dream Chain (Oswald Path)

1. **Part 1:** You dream of a casino bar
2. **Part 2:** You see a doppelganger of yourself at a Blackjack table
3. **Part 3:** Money rains from the ceiling

These dreams explore your gambling addiction and sense of identity.

### All Dreams Complete

When all three mechanics' dream chains are finished:
- Full identity revelation: "You know who you are"
- +100 HP restored
- "Lucky" status granted

## Marvin's Mystical Merchandise

### Marvin (NPC)

A quirky, mystical merchant who describes every item with elaborate, humorous adjectives.

**Availability:** 10% chance of appearing in the shop list on any given day (rare visit).

### Inventory

| Item | Availability | Notable |
|------|-------------|---------|
| All 17 upgradeable base items | 40% each | Random selection available |
| Enchanting Silver Bar | 40% | Mystical properties |
| **Animal Whistle** | 10% | $50,000 — Auto-befriends any companion |

**Pricing:** Randomized with Marvin's signature adjective descriptions. Prices include a humorous markup.

**Marvin's Adjectives:** He uses elaborate descriptors drawn from a pool of 30+ adjectives to describe items (e.g., "This TRANSCENDENTLY magnificent Golden Watch...").

## Grimy Gus's Pawn Emporium

### Gus (NPC)

A greasy pawn shop owner with a dark side. He runs "The Garble Machine."

**Availability:** Rank 3+ (Rich tier)

### Services

**Selling Items:**
- Sell any item from the pawn shop price list
- Price modifier based on your **pawn shop reputation** (0–100, starts at 50)
- Each sale gives +2 reputation

**The Garble Machine:**
- Grind items into **GRIME** (collectible currency)
- Each item produces a unique Gus description when garbled

**Collectible Completion:**
- 60+ collectible items across all adventure categories
- Selling ALL unique collectibles earns **"Gus's Precious Grime"** trophy item

### The Dark Path — Companion Selling

**Requires:** 3+ active companions.

Gus will buy your **companions**. This triggers a progressive horror storyline:

| Companions Sold | Revelation |
|----------------|------------|
| 1 | "Processing plant" mentioned |
| 3 | "Meat cubes" — you learn what happens to them |
| 5 | Pet food production details |
| 7 | "Efficient processing" — Gus describes flavors |
| 10 | Mass production — factory business card |
| **15** | **The Factory ending** — You become part of the machine. Dealer delivers 15 unique haunting betrayal lines. Death scene. Game over. |

> **Warning:** This is a point of no return. Once you start selling companions, the story gets progressively darker and can't be undone.

## Vinnie's Loan Shark

### Vinnie (NPC)

A loan shark who operates from a black sedan. His enforcer is **Tony** — a violent man who collects debts physically.

**Availability:** Rank 1+

### Loan Terms

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Maximum loan | $5,000 total |
| Interest rate | 20% per week (compound) |
| Cash type | Fraudulent (must be blended through gambling) |

### Repayment

You can repay in full, half, or fixed amounts when visiting Vinnie.

### Escalation System

Vinnie's patience runs out over time if you don't pay:

| Warning Level | What Happens |
|--------------|--------------|
| **0 — None** | Friendly greetings |
| **1 — Warning** | Phone threats, reminders (−5 sanity) |
| **2 — Threat** | Surveillance photos, Tony mentioned (−12 sanity) |
| **3 — Violence** | Tony beats you (−35 HP, −15 sanity, injuries) |
| **4 — Death Territory** | Final confrontation: pay, fight, or beg |

### Tony Fight (Level 4)

If you choose to **fight** Tony:
- **20%:** You win — receive $500 + Tony's Gun
- **30%:** You escape — barely alive
- **50%:** Tony beats you down

### Violence Outcomes (Level 4)

| Chance | Result |
|--------|--------|
| 30% | Survive barely — Broken Ribs, Concussion, Scarred |
| 40% | Lose a finger — debt halved as "payment" |
| 30% | **Death** — beaten to death by Tony |

> **Strategy:** Pay off loans quickly. The interest compounds weekly, and by level 3, you're losing HP and sanity every day.

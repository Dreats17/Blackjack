SECTION 2: TOM'S TRUCKS & TIRES (Mechanic)
================================================================================

LOCATION: Unlocked after "Tom the Mechanic" story event (Rank 1)
OWNER: Tom (friendly mechanic, key story NPC)
HOURS: 8 AM - 6 PM (day visits only)
RELATIONSHIP: Starts at 50, can increase through visits

TOM'S CHARACTER:
- Mid-40s, grease-stained overalls
- Kind-hearted, genuine
- Gives you Rebecca's phone number (critical story item)
- One of three mechanics (Tom, Frank, Oswald)
- Choosing Tom path = Family/Salvation ending track

SERVICES & PRICES:

CAR REPAIRS:
• Diagnostic Check - $30 (identifies problem)
• Oil Change - $40
• New Battery - $50
• Flat Tire Repair - $40
• New Tire - $60
• Brake Repair - $150
• Alternator Replacement - $200
• Transmission Work - $800-$1,500 (expensive)
• Engine Work - $500-$2,000 (varies)
• General Repair - $50-$300 (depends on issue)

PARTS & SUPPLIES:
• Spare Tire - $60
• Jumper Cables - $35
• Coolant - $15
• Oil Change Kit - $25
• Basic Tool Kit - $50
• Sunshade - $15

SPECIAL:
• Free Advice - Tom gives tips on car maintenance
• Payment Plans - If you're broke, Tom might let you pay later
• Story Events - Tom is key to Salvation ending

TOM'S RELATIONSHIP SYSTEM:

Relationship 0-25 (Stranger):
• "Can I help you?"
• Professional but distant

Relationship 25-50 (Acquaintance):
• "Back again? What's the trouble?"
• Starts to recognize you

Relationship 50-75 (Friend):
• "Hey! Good to see you."
• Offers discounts (5-10% off)

Relationship 75-100 (Close Friend):
• "There's my friend! Car giving you trouble?"

• Sometimes works for free
• "Don't worry about paying. We're friends."

INCREASE RELATIONSHIP:
• Each visit: +2
• Pay in full: +1
• Accept help: +3
• Talk about life: +5
• Refuse to call Rebecca: -10 (he's disappointed)
• Accept Rebecca's call: +20 (he's happy for you)

TOM'S DREAM SEQUENCES:
After building relationship, Tom appears in dreams:

1. TOM DREAM #1 - "Rebecca"
   • Tom talks about family
   • Shows you photo of his wife
   • "Family is everything, friend."
   • Subtle hint to call Rebecca

2. TOM DREAM #2 - "Nathan"
   • Tom mentions his son Nathan
   • "He's in college now. I'm so proud."
   • Reminds you of your own family

3. TOM DREAM #3 - "Johnathan"  
   • Tom reveals he had another son
   • Johnathan died in accident
   • "Don't wait. Call them. Before it's too late."
   • Powerful emotional moment

[Visit Tom]
Tom: "What brings you in today?"

1. "My car needs work" → Repair menu
2. "Just wanted to talk" → Conversation
3. "About Rebecca..." → Story progression

Options:
A. "How did you end up here?" → Tom's backstory
B. "Do you ever regret anything?" → Deep conversation
C. "Tell me about your family" → Family talk
D. "Never mind" → Return

[If select "Tell me about your family"]
Tom: "Well, I've got my wife Sandra, been married 22 years. And my son Nathan, he's in college studying engineering. I'm real proud of him."

Tom: "I had another son too. Johnathan. He... passed away. Car accident. Five years ago."

[Tom looks at you seriously]

SECTION 5: GUS'S PAWN SHOP (Grimy Gus)
================================================================================

LOCATION: Accessible after finding it (random event or exploration)
OWNER: Gus (creepy pawnbroker)
HOURS: Noon - 10 PM
REPUTATION SYSTEM: 0-100 (affects prices)

GUS'S CHARACTER:
- Greasy, unsettling
- Always watching
- Speaks in garbled mix of English and gibberish
- Has dark reputation
- Buys ANYTHING (including companions - dark path)

GUS'S SPEECH PATTERN:
Gus uses "Garble System" - random gibberish mixed with English:
• "Ah, glorpnik! Welcome, frelp customer!"
• "What you sklonk today, yes?"
• "I buy, I skrell, I zorbulate business!"

The worse your reputation, the more garbled his speech becomes.

SERVICES:

BUYING FROM YOU:
• Collectibles - Varies by item ($10-$1,000)
• Stolen goods - 50% market value
• Items - 60% retail value
• Companions - $50-$500 (DARK OPTION)

SELLING TO YOU:
• Used items - 80% retail (good deals)
• Collectibles - Random stock
• Mysterious items - Unknown effects
• "Special" items - Cursed/blessed items

REPUTATION SYSTEM:

Reputation 0-25 (Hated):
• Gus: "Glorbnix HATE you! Skrell prices UP!"
• Pays 50% less
• Charges 50% more
• Hostile

Reputation 25-50 (Disliked):
• Gus: "You... frelp okay. Normal zorbulate price."
• Normal prices
• Limited stock

Reputation 50-75 (Neutral):
• Gus: "Sklonk regular customer! Glorpnik discount!"
• 10% better prices
• More stock

Reputation 75-95 (Liked):
• Gus: "Frelp GOOD customer! Zorbulate special deal!"
• 20% better prices
• Best stock
• Special items available

Reputation 95-100 (Best Customer):
• Gus: "YOU! Glorbnix FAVORITE! Skrell anything!"
• 30% better prices
• Secret items available
• Will buy ANYTHING

INCREASE REPUTATION:
• Sell items to Gus: +2 per transaction
• Sell collectibles: +5 per collectible
• Sell companions: +10 per companion (DARK)
• Buy from Gus: +1 per purchase

DECREASE REPUTATION:
• Steal from shop: -50 (if caught)
• Insult Gus: -10
• Refuse to sell: -2

DARK PATH: SELLING COMPANIONS TO GUS

This is the BETRAYAL PATH leading to "The Factory" ending.

[Talk to Gus about companions]

Gus: "Yes yes! Frelp companions! I pay GOOD zorbulate money!"
Gus: "Dogs, skrell cats, glorpnik birds... anything!"

Options:
→ "How much?" - See prices
→ "No way!" - Refuse (moral choice)
→ "I'll think about it" - Delay

• Dogs (Lucky, etc.) - $200
• Cats (Whiskers, etc.) - $150

[Gus smiles creepily]

SHOP STRATEGIES & TIPS
================================================================================

EARLY GAME (Days 1-20):
   → Focus on Kyle's Convenience Store for basics
   → Use gas stations for cheap healing
   → Visit Tom's Garage when car breaks
   → Avoid loans if possible

MID GAME (Days 21-60):
   → Unlock pawn shop for item trading
   → Find witch doctor for cheaper healing
   → Start collecting rare items
   → Build NPC relationships for discounts

LATE GAME (Days 61+):
   → Use high-tier shops (doctor, casino services)
   → Complete NPC questlines for special items
   → Prepare for ending choices (airport, etc.)
   → Max out all shop relationships

MONEY SAVING TIPS:
   ✓ Buy in bulk when possible
   ✓ Haggle with friendly NPCs
   ✓ Use gas stations instead of restaurants
   ✓ Collect free items before buying
   ✓ Build relationships for discounts (10-30% off)
   ✓ Complete quests for free items/services

BEST VALUE SHOPS:
   1. Gas Station - Cheapest healing
   2. Kyle's Convenience - Best daily items
   3. Witch Doctor - Cheap potions (if unlocked)
   4. Thrift Store - Bargain collectibles
   5. Pawn Shop - Fair prices for selling

AVOID THESE TRAPS:
   ✗ Loan Shark (unless you can repay fast)
   ✗ Casino Credit (debt spiral)
   ✗ Overpriced casino shop items
   ✗ Drug dealer (addiction path)
   ✗ Buying items you can find free

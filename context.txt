1. WHAT IS THIS GAME?
================================================================================
A text-based roguelike RPG disguised as a Blackjack card game. The player is
stranded with $50, discovers an abandoned casino with a mysterious Dealer, and
must gamble their way to $1,000,000 to escape. Along the way they experience
hundreds of random narrative events, collect items, adopt animal companions,
manage health/sanity/fatigue, visit shops, complete multi-part storylines, and
ultimately reach one of 10+ endings.

Platform: Windows terminal (uses msvcrt for key input)
Language: Python 3
Dependencies: colorama (terminal colors), msvcrt (Windows keyboard), time, random, sys
No database, no save system, no networking. Everything is in-memory per session.

2. TONE, PURPOSE & WRITING STYLE
================================================================================

THE CORE IDENTITY:
This game is NOT a fun lighthearted card game. It is a survival horror roguelike
wearing a card game as a mask. The player is TRAPPED. They are homeless, stranded,
sleeping in a broken car, and gambling under the watch of an ancient, possibly
immortal Dealer who will literally shoot them dead if they refuse to play. The
entire experience is about being ground down — by poverty, by addiction, by
loneliness, by a world that doesn't care — and finding meaning (or not) in that.

THE TONAL BALANCE — THIS IS CRITICAL:
The game oscillates between two modes constantly. Getting this balance right is
the single most important thing when writing new content:

  MODE 1: ABSURD DARK COMEDY (60% of events)
    - Deadpan observational humor about being miserable
    - The player's suffering is described with casual, almost bored detachment
    - Absurd situations played completely straight
    - Self-deprecating internal monologue
    - Examples of the voice:
      * "Your car starts, sputters, coughs, and dies. It sounds like it's given up on life. You relate."
      * "You walk face-first into your own side mirror. Ow."
      * "After ten minutes of wandering in circles, you find it again. That was embarrassing."
      * "You wish for a million dollars. Obviously."
      * "Old Man Jenkins rants for a solid ten minutes about the good old days of homelessness."
      * "The Raccoon Mafia wants tribute."
      * A mime silently acts out your sad life, including a dramatic death scene
      * You carry a grandfather clock in your pocket and the Dealer mocks you for it
      * The Dealer: "Is that a grandfather clock in your pocket, or are you just happy to see me? Wait. That's actually a grandfather clock. Why do you have that."

  MODE 2: GENUINE DARKNESS AND EMOTIONAL WEIGHT (40% of events, all endings)
    - When the game goes dark, it goes DARK. No jokes. No relief. Just raw.
    - The Dealer's dog story: He shot his dog Scrappy in the woods after it bit his face off. "Even still, he kept whimpering. I couldn't bear to watch him die, so I just drove off."
    - Holocaust references: The Dealer is a Holocaust survivor. "I've seen it before. In Germany. In Poland. In a hundred little towns just like this one, where men with small hearts and loud voices convinced themselves they had the right to decide who lives and who dies."
    - Frank is revealed as a neo-Nazi who tries to recruit you. You can shoot him.
    - The Retribution ending: "Six shots. Six bodies. Six men who thought they were gods, reduced to meat on the floor."
    - The painkiller/Dr. Feelgood storylines deal directly with drug addiction, dependency, and withdrawal
    - The Madness ending: Your own shadow takes over your body. "Cold fingers wrap around your throat. Your own fingers. Your own hands."
    - The Broken state hallucinations: "You can hear the slot machines screaming. No one else seems to notice." / "Every patron in the casino has your face. You blink. They don't anymore. Did they ever?"
    - The Eternity ending: You become a cyborg-god and crush Oswald's skull with your bare hands. "Gray matter. Blood. Bone fragments."
    - Suicide and self-harm implications: "You notice blood on your hands. You don't remember how it got there."
    - Companion death causes massive sanity loss. Selling companions to the pawn shop is explicitly framed as betrayal.

THE TRANSITION BETWEEN MODES:
The game does NOT signal when it's shifting. A silly raccoon event can be
immediately followed by a sanity hallucination. The Dealer can joke about your
hat one line and threaten to kill you the next. This whiplash IS the tone.
Never telegraph that something dark is coming. Never apologize for it after.
The player should constantly feel slightly off-balance — is this funny? Is this
scary? Is this... both?

THE DEALER'S VOICE:
  - Always in red text, always type.slow()
  - Speaks in complete sentences, often poetic or philosophical
  - Dry, cutting wit — not sarcastic, just brutally honest
  - Knows more than he lets on. Every line implies centuries of experience.
  - Can be menacing: "Well that's just too bad, isn't it." *shoots you*
  - Can be tender: "That... was unexpected." (voice different, softer, almost warm)
  - Can be darkly funny: "Wind didn't blow any of your money away, did it? Anyways, let's play."
  - Refers to himself obliquely. Smokes cigarettes. Carries a revolver always.
  - His dialogue has weight — he doesn't waste words. Short sentences when serious.
  - When he reveals his past (rare), his speech becomes longer, more vulnerable, almost pleading for understanding

THE NARRATOR VOICE (second-person):
  - "You" is the only pronoun. Never "I" unless quoting the player.
  - Flat, observational, slightly detached — like a nature documentary about your suffering
  - Mixes clinical description with sudden emotional gut-punches
  - Uses present tense for immediacy: "You watch as he stands" not "You watched"
  - Loves a good trailing sentence: "You lay down, and close your eyes. It's time to rest."
  - Internal thoughts expressed without "you think": just stated as fact
  - Physical sensations described in detail (warmth of bourbon, cold of the Dealer's hand)
  - The broken/low-sanity narrator becomes UNRELIABLE — contradicts itself, second-guesses reality:
    "The casino is upside down. No. It's not. It never was. You're fine. You're fine."

DIALOGUE FORMAT:
  - Player speech uses escaped quotes: quote("Let's get this over with.")
  - Dealer speech uses red text: type.slow(red("How much for this first hand?"))
  - NPC speech uses regular quotes: type.type(quote("Excuse me! We're trying to find the highway?"))
  - The game has very little dialogue overall — most is narration. When characters speak, it matters.
  - Speech tends to be clipped and natural, not flowery: "Thanks, yeah, that would be great."

THINGS THE TONE IS NOT:
  - Not ironic or meta (the game never winks at the player or breaks the 4th wall)
  - Not grimdark for shock value (the violence serves the story, it's never gratuitous without purpose)
  - Not uniformly nihilistic — but the nihilism is real, frequent, and often the point. The game's deepest trick
    is using absurdist comedy as a delivery mechanism for genuine darkness. The best example is Phil (the interrogator
    storyline): he begins as a pure joke — a man in a bright red suit with a fake FBI badge written in Comic Sans,
    staring at the sun for 30 seconds before driving away. Four visits later, he's crying on the ground begging for
    his family while a freight truck crushes him. The narration's response: "Phil might've been crazy, but at least
    he was consistent." No sanity loss. No consequences. No lesson. You push his car into a lake and the game moves
    on. Similarly, Betsy the cow ends events with "You won, Betsy, you won." Some endings offer genuine redemption
    (Retribution, Salvation). Others offer none. Many events have no lesson and no silver lining — things just happen
    to you, and the world doesn't care. The nihilism is not edgy or decorative. It's structural: the narration treats
    horror and comedy with identical flatness, and THAT is the joke and the horror simultaneously.
  - Not comedic at the expense of real suffering (the addiction storylines are treated with respect)
  - Not a parody of anything — the card game is real, the stakes are real, the emotions are real

COLOR/FORMATTING CONVENTIONS:
  - Dealer speech: type.slow(red("..."))
  - Danger/damage: red text
  - Money/positive: green text + bright
  - Base items: magenta + bright, via item("Item Name") helper
  - Upgraded items: cyan + bright
  - Day/rank info: yellow + bright
  - Sanity effects/telepathy: cyan (not bright)
  - Broken hallucinations: red
  - Drama/emphasis: bright() wrapper
  - Status announcements: yellow

WHEN WRITING NEW CONTENT — CHECKLIST:
  □ Does it sound like it could follow "Your car starts, sputters, coughs, and dies"?
  □ If it's funny, is the humor deadpan and observational, not wacky or slapstick?
  □ If it's dark, does it trust the reader to feel the weight without telling them how to feel?
  □ Does the Dealer sound like a centuries-old gunslinger, not a sarcastic video game NPC?
  □ Is the narrator flat and observational, not melodramatic?
  □ Does it end with print("\n") after the last line of text?
  □ If there's a choice, does it use ask.yes_or_no() or numbered menu?
  □ Does it use the correct color functions for the context?

4. CLASS ARCHITECTURE — THE MIXIN PATTERN
================================================================================
The Player class is built from 25 mixins. This is the ONLY inheritance pattern.
Defined in player_core.py:

class Player(
    SystemsMixin,           # Companions, achievements, gambling stats, loan shark, pawn shop, dealer happiness, gifts
    EconomyMixin,           # Balance, rank, selling
    DayCycleMixin,          # End day summary, opening, night start, dream dispatch
    # ── Day event mixins (11 themed files) ──
    DaySurvivalMixin,       # Health, car, weather, food, basic hardship events
    DayPeopleMixin,         # Stranger encounters, NPCs, social events
    DayAnimalsMixin,        # Animal encounters, companion opportunities
    MechanicsStorylineMixin,# Tom/Frank/Oswald first meetings + full dream chains
    DayCasinoMixin,         # Casino-specific encounters
    DayWealthMixin,         # Wealth-tier events, luxury/poverty extremes
    DayDarkMixin,           # Horror, violence, trauma, addiction events
    DayCompanionsMixin,     # Companion-focused day events
    DayItemsMixin,          # Item-find and item-interaction events
    DaySurrealMixin,        # Paranormal, absurd, surreal events
    DayNumbersMixin,        # Secret events gated by exact balance/day/stat values
    DayStorylinesMixin,     # Events tied to multi-part storylines
    # ── Other event domains ──
    IllnessMixin,           # Injuries, illness, doctor/witch doctor
    CarEventsMixin,         # Random car trouble events
    NightEventsMixin,       # ~100+ random night events
    AdventuresMixin,        # Beach/Forest/Mountain/City adventure content
    GameFlowMixin,          # start_day(), daily item effects, pest control, car trouble
    LocationsMixin,         # afternoon() location choices, all shop interactions
    DurabilityMixin,        # Item durability tracking
    EventDispatchMixin,     # day_event()/night_event() dispatchers
    EndingsMixin,           # All ending sequences
):

All mixins access player state via self._ prefixed attributes (single underscore, "protected").
The Blackjack class is SEPARATE — it holds the card game logic and has a reference to the Player via self.__player.
lists.py Lists class takes a player reference and builds all event/dialogue pools.

5. CORE GAME LOOP
================================================================================
In blackjackMain.py:

    player = story.Player()
    blackjackGame = blackjack.Blackjack(player)
    player.opening_lines()           # Intro narrative, first night
    while True:
        blackjackGame.play_round()   # Gambling session (3-4 hands of blackjack)
        player.end_day()             # Day summary OR dream event
        player.start_day()           # Morning: companion updates, loan shark, achievements, item effects
        player.afternoon()           # Location choices: shops, companions, adventure areas, OR travel restrictions

Detailed flow per cycle:
1. MORNING (start_day in game_flow.py):
   - "Morning, Day X" banner
   - update_companions_daily() — hunger decay, happiness checks, runaway risk
   - update_loan_shark_daily() — interest, warning escalation
   - check_achievements()
   - Daily item effects (Suzy's Gift heals sanity, Necronomicon corrupts, Cursed Coin misfortune, etc.)
   - Pest control check (cockroaches steal money if no Pest Control item)
   - Car trouble check
   - Status display (health bar, balance, rank)
   - day_event() — Random event from wealth-rank-appropriate pool
   - Storyline check — StorylineSystem may fire a multi-part narrative event

2. AFTERNOON (afternoon in locations.py):
   - If travel restriction (Wind, Rain, Battery, Engine, Wasted Afternoon): skip choices
   - If millionaire + visitor came: millionaire_afternoon() (special ending path choices)
   - If companions exist: option to spend time with them
   - Otherwise: numbered menu of available locations:
     * Doctor's Office, Witch Doctor's Tower, mechanics (Tom/Frank/Oswald), Convenience Store,
       Marvin's shop, Pawn Shop, Loan Shark, Phone Calls, Car Workbench, Airport, adventure areas
     * Or "Stay Home"
   - Player picks a number, goes there, interacts

3. EVENING/NIGHT (start_night in day_cycle.py):
   - Dealer welcome line (red text)
   - Broken state / low sanity flavor text
   - Grandfather Clock joke dialogue (rare)

4. GAMBLING (play_round in blackjack.py):
   - update_player() syncs balance, resets per-session item flags
   - Item announcement messages (watches, gloves, cloaks, etc.)
   - Loop for 3-4 hands (based on items):
     * Player places bet
     * Cards dealt, player hits/stands/doubles/splits/surrenders
     * Items activate during play (peek at dealer card, insurance, lucky coin, etc.)
     * Win/lose/push resolved, balance updated, gambling stats tracked
   - Broken state: hallucination text before gambling

5. END OF DAY (end_day in day_cycle.py):
   - Check for dream events (replace summary with narrative dream sequences)
   - OR show day summary: days survived, balance change, current total, rank comment, advice, quote
   - Heal 1-5 HP
   - night_event() — Random night event
   - Fatigue increase
   - Companion night bonuses

7. EVENT SYSTEM
================================================================================
Events are METHOD NAMES stored as strings in lists. The Lists class builds
shuffled pools per wealth rank. EventDispatchMixin.day_event() calls:
    dayEvent = getattr(self, self._lists.get_day_event())
    dayEvent()

So an event like "find_20_dollars" is a method on the Player class (via DayEventsMixin).
When a pool is exhausted, it rebuilds. Events are shuffled so order is random.

Event pools by rank:
  Poor (0):        ~50 day events, ~20 night events
  Cheap (1):       ~70 day events, ~25 night events
  Modest (2):      ~70 day events, ~25 night events
  Rich (3):        ~60 day events, ~20 night events
  Doughman (4):    ~60 day events, ~20 night events
  Nearly There (5):~50 day events, ~20 night events

Events can: change balance, change health, change sanity, add/remove items,
add/remove status effects, add injuries, trigger NPC meetings, start storylines,
kill the player, adopt companions, and more. Most events are self-contained
methods 10-80 lines long with typewriter text output.

Secret events: Some events check exact conditions before running (exact balance
amounts like $420, $7777, $69420; palindrome days; prime days; health=balance;
etc.). If conditions aren't met, they silently redirect to a normal event.

8. STORYLINE SYSTEM (storylines.py)
================================================================================
StorylineSystem manages 20+ multi-part sequential narratives. Each storyline:
  - Has multiple stages (1-5+)
  - Has a base_chance (10-40%) that escalates each day by escalation_rate (6-25%)
  - Has a min_gap between stages (1-5 days)
  - Has start_conditions (balance thresholds, items owned, NPCs met, etc.)
  - Fires during start_day() after the random day event
  - Only one storyline can fire per day
  - Progress is tracked per-storyline (current stage, days since last)

Key storylines:
  the_debt_collector     — Loan shark threats escalate
  the_wandering_musician — Mysterious musician appears, teaches lessons
  the_carnival           — Carnival comes to town, games and prizes
  the_collectors_eye     — Rich collector wants to buy your items
  franks_garage          — Frank's dark past unfolds
  the_dealers_past       — Dealer's mysterious history revealed
  rabbit_chase           — 7-part chain chasing a rabbit through the woods
  the_mechanic_rivalry   — Tom vs Frank vs Oswald drama
  the_graveyard          — Spooky cemetery discoveries
  painkiller             — Substance abuse storyline (requires injury)
  dr_feelgood            — Shady doctor experiments (requires severe injury)
  the_heist              — Planning and executing a casino heist
  the_revival            — Religious awakening
  the_conspiracy         — Strange patterns in the casino
  the_gambler_ghost      — Ghost of a past gambler haunts you

================================================================================
9. COMPANION SYSTEM (systems.py + lists.py)
================================================================================
Companions are animals that travel with you. Stored in self._companions dict.

Acquisition: Random events (day/night), specific storylines, purchases
Each companion has: name, type, happiness (0-100), days_owned, fed_today, bonded (bool)

Daily cycle:
  - Morning: update_companions_daily() — if not fed yesterday: -5 happiness. If happiness ≤10: 33% chance runs away.
  - Afternoon: "Spend time with companions?" option. Can feed, pet, interact individually or as group.
  - Night: apply_companion_night_bonuses() — Patches gives sanity at night.

Bonding: happiness ≥80 + owned 7+ days → bonded = True. Unique dialogue.

Companion types defined in lists.py make_companion_types():
  Squirrelly (Squirrel), Whiskers (Cat), Lucky (Dog), Mr. Pecks (Crow),
  Patches (Opossum), Rusty (Raccoon), Slick (Snake), Hopper (Rabbit)
  Plus rare ones found in events: Echo (Dolphin), Kraken, Moonwhisker (Magical Rabbit),
  Shellbert (Tortoise), Don (Raccoon Boss), Grace (Deer), and others.

Each type has 4 mood categories × 4-6 dialogue lines.

Companion bonuses (daily, chance-based):
  - Sanity restore: +1 to +10 depending on companion type
  - Money find: Mr. Pecks (5%, $1-20), Rusty (3%, $2-15)
  - Luck bonus: Hopper, Squirrelly
  - Danger warning: Whiskers
  - Night healing: Patches

Dark path: Sell companions to Grimy Gus at the pawn shop. Selling 3+ triggers dark achievement.

10. ITEM SYSTEM

CASINO ITEMS (bought at Marvin's, found in events, 17 upgradeable):
  Each has a base version and an upgraded version (upgraded at Oswald's after 3+ mechanic visits).
  Items affect gameplay during blackjack hands and passive daily effects.

  Base → Upgraded:
  Delight Indicator → Delight Manipulator    (Dealer happiness visibility/control)
  Health Indicator → Health Manipulator       (Health visibility/control)
  Dirty Old Hat → Unwashed Hair              (Lowers minimum bets)
  Golden Watch → Sapphire Watch              (Extra gambling round)
  Sneaky Peeky Shades → Sneaky Peeky Goggles (Peek at Dealer's hole card)
  Quiet Sneakers → Quiet Bunny Slippers      (Stealth bonuses)
  Faulty Insurance → Real Insurance           (Loss protection)
  Lucky Coin → Lucky Medallion               (Luck boost)
  Worn Gloves → Velvet Gloves                (Card handling)
  Tattered Cloak → Invisible Cloak           (Avoidance)

11. ENDINGS
================================================================================
The game has 10+ distinct endings, triggered by different paths:

MILLIONAIRE PATH ($1,000,000+):
  1. Hit $1M → _is_millionaire = True
  2. Next morning: Special visitor arrives, tells you to visit a mechanic
  3. Afternoon: Choose which mechanic to visit (Tom, Frank, or Oswald)
  4. Each mechanic path branches into sub-endings:

  TOM PATH:
    - Salvation: Tom tells you to go to church. Religious healing ending.
    - Resurrection: Tom tells you about the Dealer's dog story. Leave the casino forever.
  
  FRANK PATH:
    - Frank is revealed as a neo-Nazi. You get a choice:
    - Destruction: Shoot the Dealer with Frank's revolver
    - Retribution: Shoot Frank, spare the Dealer. Dealer reveals his name is Mortimer, 
      gives you a jade chip from Vienna 1938.
  
  OSWALD PATH:
    - Transcendence: Accept Oswald's offer to take over the casino. You become the new Dealer.
    - Eternity: Requires ALL 17 items fully upgraded. You transcend into something beyond human.
  
  AIRPORT:
    - Bliss: Fly away to a tropical island. Simple happy ending.
    - Sanctuary: Requires 5+ companions + Animal Whistle. Charter flight, live on wildlife reserve.

SANITY ENDINGS:
  - Madness: Sanity hits 0 (40% chance). Shadow confrontation with 3 philosophical questions.
    Score 5+/9 to survive. Fail = shadow consumes you.
  - Broken: Sanity hits 0 (60% chance). Not a true ending — game continues but with permanent
    hallucinations, unreliable narration, random gameplay effects.

DEATH:
  - Health reaches 0
  - Dealer kills you (happiness = 0)
  - Loan shark sends Tony
  - Say "no" on Day 1
  - Various lethal events

DREAM CHAINS (prerequisites for some endings):
  Tom dreams:    rebecca → nathan → johnathan (at $1k, $10k, $500k)
  Frank dreams:  dealers_anger → dealers_scar → dealers_revolver → dealer_in_dreams
  Oswald dreams: casino_bar → casino_table → casino_riches
  30% chance per night to fire if eligible. Replace day summary with dream narrative.

13. TEXT OUTPUT SYSTEM (typer.py)
================================================================================
Type class methods:
  type.fast(text)        — Medium-speed typewriter. Main narration speed.
  type.slow(text)        — Slow typewriter. Used for dramatic moments, Dealer speech.
  type.fast_clean(text)  — Like fast but doesn't pause on colons.
  type.type(text)        — Standard speed. General text.
  type.typeover(prompt, text, newline) — Type over previous text (for banners).

Speed control (during any typewriter output):
  SPACEBAR = skip to end instantly
  COMMA (,) = Default speed
  PERIOD (.) = Fast speed
  SLASH (/) = Fastest speed
  P = Print mode (instant, no animation)

Ask class methods:
  ask.yes_or_no(prompt)     — Returns "yes" or "no"
  ask.press_continue(prompt) — Waits for any key
  ask.number(prompt, max)   — Returns number 1-max

COLOR FUNCTIONS (defined in EVERY file, duplicated everywhere — this is intentional):
  red(text)     = Fore.RED      — Dealer speech, danger, damage
  green(text)   = Fore.GREEN    — Money gains, positive
  magenta(text) = Fore.MAGENTA  — Base items
  yellow(text)  = Fore.YELLOW   — Day/rank info, warnings
  cyan(text)    = Fore.CYAN     — Upgraded items, sanity effects
  bright(text)  = Style.BRIGHT  — Emphasis
  italic(text)  = Style.DIM     — Subtle text
  item(text)    = magenta(bright(text)) — Item display shorthand
  quote(text)   = wraps in double quotes

NOTE: Every single mixin file re-defines these color helper functions at the top.
This is a deliberate pattern, not a bug. Don't try to "fix" it by centralizing them.

14. KEY PATTERNS & CONVENTIONS
================================================================================

EVENT METHOD NAMING:
  - Day events: descriptive snake_case (find_20_dollars, creepy_stranger, dog_adoption)
  - Night events: similar (stargazing, bar_fight, mysterious_letter)
  - Storyline events: prefixed chain (carnival_stage_1, carnival_stage_2, etc.)
  - Events are referenced by string name in lists, called via getattr()

STATE CHECKS:
  self.has_item("Item Name")         — Check inventory
  self.has_met("NPC Name")           — Check if NPC encountered
  self.has_danger("Danger Name")     — Check active dangers
  self.has_travel_restriction("X")   — Check movement blocks
  self.has_flask_effect("Effect")    — Check active flask effects
  self.has_status_effect("Effect")   — Check status effects

STATE MUTATIONS:
  self.add_item("Item Name")         — Add to inventory
  self.use_item("Item Name")         — Remove from inventory
  self.meet("NPC Name")              — Mark NPC as met
  self.add_danger("Danger Name")     — Add danger
  self.lose_danger("Danger Name")    — Remove danger
  self.hurt(amount)                  — Reduce health (can trigger death/LifeAlert)
  self.heal(amount)                  — Increase health (caps at 100)
  self.change_balance(amount)        — Add/subtract money with formatted display
  self.change_sanity(amount)         — Adjust sanity
  self.add_fatigue(amount)           — Increase fatigue

PRINTING PATTERN:
  type.type("Narrative text here.")
  print("\n")                        — Blank line between paragraphs
  type.slow(red("Dealer dialogue.")) — Dealer always in red, always slow

USER INPUT PATTERN:
  answer = ask.yes_or_no("Question? ")
  if answer == "yes": ...

MENU PATTERN:
  type.type("1. Option A")
  print()
  type.type("2. Option B")
  print()
  type.type("Choose: ")
  # Then manual input parsing or ask.number()

15. IMPORTANT GOTCHAS FOR AI AGENTS
================================================================================

1. DO NOT centralize color functions. They are intentionally duplicated in every file.
   This is a design choice, not technical debt.

2. The game has NO save system. Each run is a fresh session. All state is in Player.__init__.

3. Events are STRINGS matching METHOD NAMES. If you add an event method, you must also
   add its string name to the appropriate list in lists.py make_*_events_list().

4. The Blackjack class and Player class are SEPARATE. Blackjack holds card game state.
   Player holds all RPG state. They communicate via self.__player reference in Blackjack.

5. msvcrt is Windows-only. This game only runs on Windows.

6. type.slow() pauses on periods, commas, exclamation marks. If you put ANSI color codes
   after punctuation, the pause still triggers on the punctuation character.

7. Items are stored as plain strings in a set. "Lucky Coin" and "Lucky Medallion" are
   different items. Upgrading removes the base and adds the upgraded version.

8. The game can reach ~$1M naturally through blackjack wins. At that point, the game
   pivots from roguelike to narrative ending selection.

9. Storylines use escalating probability. A storyline with 10% base and 8% escalation
   will be at 10%, 18%, 26%, 34%... each day until it fires, then resets for next stage.

10. has_met() is used for EVERYTHING — NPC meetings, milestone tracking, quest flags,
    one-time event gates. It's basically the global flag system.

11. The Dealer's real name is Mortimer. He's possibly centuries old, survived the
    Holocaust, has a jade glass eye and a scar from a dog bite. This is revealed
    only in the Retribution ending.

12. When adding new events: follow the existing pattern. Create a method on the
    appropriate mixin, add the string name to the lists, use type.type/type.slow
    for output, use the color functions, end with print("\n").

13. Achievement names are stored as strings in a set. The check_achievements() method
    in systems.py scans game state and unlocks them. ~240+ achievements exist across
    categories: gambling, survival, companion, economic, collection, social, secret.

14. The _rank property (0-5) determines which event pool is used. Events get more
    complex/dangerous/rewarding at higher ranks. Each rank has its own day AND night
    event pool in lists.py.

15. The game's humor comes from CONTRAST — absurd comedy (grandfather clock in pocket)
    juxtaposed with genuine emotional weight (Holocaust survivor backstory, addiction
    storylines). New content should maintain this tonal balance.

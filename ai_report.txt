CURRENT DIAGNOSIS

The handler now has two distinct quality levels.

Tier 1: escaped runs
- Can use Vinnie.
- Can reach Marvin.
- Can occasionally hit rank 2 and five-figure bankrolls.
- Still lacks debt cleanup, late threat management, and rich-run stabilization.

Tier 2: the majority of runs
- Never reach stable car ownership.
- Spend too much of the run in health and sanity emergencies.
- Die or break before Marvin and late systems matter.

The next iteration should focus on moving more runs from Tier 2 into Tier 1.

WHAT TO DO NEXT

Priority 1: strengthen the pre-car and early post-car phase.

Concrete targets:
- Raise ever_car from 37/100 back above 43/100.
- Raise rank1+ from 21/100 back above 28/100.
- Cut no-car runs from 64/100 materially.

Likely code targets:
- tools/quicktest.py
- tools/autoplay/planner.py
- tools/autoplay/state.py
- tools/autoplay/policies/blackjack_policy.py

Specific changes to try:
- Make early survival more conservative before first car.
- Hold cleaner reserves around mechanic affordability bands.
- Reduce avoidable doctor churn immediately after car acquisition.
- Add a stricter post-car fragile phase that suppresses optional shopping unless the purchase is explicitly phase-correct.
- Keep rank 0 and fragile rank 1 runs from taking soft-value store detours.

Priority 2: add debt-exit discipline after successful loan pushes.

Evidence says the push works, but the cleanup does not.

Specific changes to try:
- Once a loan-funded Marvin buy succeeds, add a debt cleanup subphase.
- Lower aggression after the purchase if the run is already at rank 1 or rank 2.
- Route to repayment before warning level 2+ becomes common.

Priority 3: improve rich-run stabilization, not richer-run aggression.

The batch already proves that some runs can reach 10k to 50k.
The issue is keeping them alive and solvent after that.

Specific changes to try:
- Threat-aware de-risking at rank 2.
- Better sanity protection once money is no longer the limiting resource.
- Stronger suppression of unnecessary gambling after major conversion wins.
